UK Government Invests £45M in 'Sunrise' AI Supercomputer for Fusion Energy

The UK government, through the Department for Energy Security and Net Zero (DESNZ), is investing £45 million to establish 'Sunrise', the country's first AI supercomputer dedicated to fusion energy, at the UK Atomic Energy Authority’s (UKAEA) Culham Campus. This initiative aims to accelerate fusion energy development by tackling key challenges in plasma turbulence, materials development, and tritium fuel breeding, aligning with the UK's Fusion Strategy and broader net-zero ambitions. Targeted for operation in June, Sunrise will deliver up to 6.76 Exaflops of AI-accelerated modelling, enabling high-fidelity simulations and the creation of digital twins for complex systems. This collaborative effort involves AMD, Dell Technologies, Intel, UKAEA, and the University of Cambridge, strengthening the UK's high-performance computing and AI capabilities.
